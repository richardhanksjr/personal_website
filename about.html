Biography

I am a software developer, musician, educator and, perhaps most importantly, a life-long student! I was born in Gardner, MA and developed an interest in music in the 5th grade. This interest quickly became a single-minded focus on becoming a professional musician. After graduating from high school, I went to Umass Amherst to study music. After earning a Master's degree in trombone performance, I went on to play professionally for 6 years in the United States Navy. My time in the Navy gave him a chance to travel internationally, play for government officials, entertain troops, perform for students, play for professional football, baseball, and hockey games, and even play at the Daytona 500.


As my time in the Navy was coming to and end, I began to think about what I might do for a new career. I took classes in educational psychology (learning and teaching are two of my most favorite interests) classes as well as premed (I'm also really interested in health) classes. Then, on a whim, I tried my hands at an online coding tutorial and I instantly fell in love. I began writing code every night after work and on the weekends.


After being honorably discharged from the Navy, I decided to continue my software development training and went to Denver, CO to study Java and web development at The Skill Distillery, a 5 month intensive coding boot camp. After graduation, I got a job as a software developer with Bank of America where I currently work as a Python/Django web developer for Global Information Security where I help develop solutions for our organization's cyber security team.


In 2016, I decided to take me software development skills to the next level and I enrolled in Boston University's Master's in Software Development program. I completed coursework for this in December, 2018 and officially graduated in 2019. Currently, I am pursuing the Web Application Development graduate certificate from BU as I continue to improve my skills as a software developer.


I am a lifelong learner and teacher. I truly enjoy the process of learning and teaching others. To that end, I am currently researching methods to improve the education of software developers and plan to create helpful materials out of that research to help new and experienced developers improve their skills
